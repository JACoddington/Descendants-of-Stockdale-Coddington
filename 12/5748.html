  General Notes:

Hornell, N.Y. \emdash

Albert J. Coddington "AJ or Uncle Al," 94, of Hill Street, passed away Tuesday (March 13, 2012) at home.
Born in Hornell, Aug. 28, 1917, the son of Bert and Ethel (Dayton) Coddington, he had resided in the Hornell area all his life. Albert served in the former Company K during the war, and in his early years drove for the Hornell Wholesale. He then worked for the Erie Railroad, and retired from the former Foster Wheeler Corp. in Dansville. He enjoyed hunting, fishing with his sons, camping and traveling. After retirement, Al liked mowing his lawn in his "tractor" and each winter he would spend time planning out the next year's garden. It would be overflowing with tomatoes, peppers and flowers. Forever a dog lover, he was never without at least one for his adult life. His last years, he had the constant companionship of his "babies," his three Pomeranians.
In addition to his parents, Al was predeceased by his wife, Beverly Jean Dunn Coddington in 2007; daughters, Marilyn Coddington Whiteman and Patti Ann Coddington; brothers, "Harold" and Robert Coddington; sister, Kathleen Stewart.
He is survived by three sons, Joseph (Beverly) Coddington of Sparta, Tenn., Thomas (Karen) Coddington of Arkport, and David (Lisa) Whiteman of Hornell; two daughters, Joyce Coddington of Hornell, and Terri Coddington Hunt of Hornell; sister, Ruth Hawkins of Tucson, Ariz.; special cousin, Jennie Mullikin of Hornell; 17 grandchildren, 30 great-grandchildren, 21 great-great-grandchildren; special niece, Ethel North Irish; several nieces and nephews.
To send a remembrance please visit www.brownandpowersfuneralhomes.com. The family is being assisted by Adam E. DuBois, director.
The family will be present to receive friends from 4 until 7 p.m. on Friday at the Bender - Brown & Powers Funeral Home, 354 Canisteo St., Hornell. Funeral services will be held at 11 a.m. on Saturday at the funeral home. Burial will be in Rural Cemetery, Hornell. 3142
