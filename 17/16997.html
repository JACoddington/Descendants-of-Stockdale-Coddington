  General Notes:

Robert Booth (pronounced "Bowth" and frequently spelled Bouth) first appears in land transactions in York County, Virginia, in 1638. He served as clerk for that county from 1639 to 1652; consequently, his name appears on several official records. His use of Latin in court records reveals him as an educated man, and he may have been a physician. He represented York County in the House of Burgesses at Jamestown in 1653 and 1654. He patented 880 acres of land for the importation of eighteen persons in 1 653, and other tracts were acquired by purchase until his land holdings were extensive. His library of three hundred volumes was valued at fourteen pounds sterling. His wife, Frances, was born in 1609, according to her 1668 deposition. (Erwin 1930,222). 6090
