  General Notes:

"He was an adventurous type of man, and had traveled widely before his marriage in 1841. He was not satisfied with life in Perth Amboy, and took his wife to Texas, where he engaged in a number of activities. Dissatisfied with Texas, he went on a voyage to Hawaii (then called the Sandwich Islands) early in 1854, and thence went to Chile with a cargo of flour. By 1855 he was in California, looking for gold. He wrote to his wife Lavinia to come from Texas to California and bring the children. She received this letter in 1855 and soon set out with her three small sons, going first to Panama by ship, and then across the Isthmus to Panama City, from which she and her sons once more sailed for California. She died of fever at sea a few days out from Panama. Meanwhile, her husband was killed by Indians in California early in March, 1856, so that when the three motherless Redmond boys arrived in San Francisco, they found that they were fatherless too. 28
