  General Notes:

During the Revolutionary War, enlisted 21 Apr. 1775 as a private, Capt. Morrell's 4th Regiment., New Jersey Continental Line, and was omitted from that unit Dec. 1777. He appeared as private in Capt. Ward's Co., Western Regiment. Morris Co. Militia. He probably also served, again as a private, in Capt. Matthew Freeman's Co., New Jersey State Troops, 1781, enlisted to serve one year. Matthew Freeman resigned due to ill health and his Lieutenant, Asher FitzRandolph succeeded him and was promoted to Captain.
He made entry of his earmarks for livestock on 9/14/ 1780, "which is a crop of the left ear and a half peny under the same and a swallow fork in the right ear."

In 1778, as a single man, was taxed £1 5s, Od.
In 1779 he owned 89 acres worth 6s per acre, 1 horse, and 1 cow, for which he was taxed £1 11s 6d.
In 1784 he owned 80 acres worth 8s per acre, 2 horses, and 8 cattle, and had 7 white and no black people in his household.
In 1785 he owned 80 acres worth 7s per acre, 1 horse, and 9 cattle, for which he was taxed £1 11s 10d.
In 1786 he owned 80 acres worth 6s 6d per acre, 1 horse, and 11 cattle, for which he was taxed £1 5s 0d.
In 1787 he owned 80 acres worth 6s 6d per acre, 2 horses, and 11 cattle, for which he was taxed £2 10s 0d.
In 1788 he owned 80 acres worth 6s 6d per acre, 2 horses, and 12 cattle, for which he was taxed £2 10s 8d.
In 1789 he owned 80 acres worth 6s 6d per acre, 2 horses, and 13 cattle, for which he was taxed £2 11s 11d.
In Perth Amboy in 1793 he owned 140 acres, 2 horses, and 12 cattle, and one carriage.
In 1797, he owned 218 acres worth 25s an acre, 2 horses, 13 cattle, 2 slaves, and 1 dog. for which he was taxed £2 12s 0d..
In 1808, his widow owned 1 horse and 2 cattle.
