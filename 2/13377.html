  General Notes:

He had churches in Illinois and South Dakota. Representative to S. Dakota legislature, Codington Co. South Dakota was named for him.
Ex HGC notes, made ca. 1896. "Son of G. C. B. April 8, 1836, d. and buried in Ann Arbor, Mich. Sept 1878 -- Cong. Minister -- Co. in South Dakota name after him also Memorial Church. A member of territorial legislature -- widow married again -- no children."

Codington, George Spencer, son of George and Minerva Elizabeth (Pease) Codington, was born at Seneca Falls, N. Y., 1838, April 8. Syracuse High School. At sea, 1857-60. Student in Michigan University, 1861-2. In the army, 1862-5. Commercial College, Indianapolis, St. Louis, 1865-7. Graduated, Chicago Theological Seminary, 1870. Ordained, evangelist, 1870, July 1, Lacon, Ill. Acting pastor there till 1871; Creston till 1872, Oct., when he removed to Dakota, where he organized churches at Dell Rapids and Medway, 'and remained in charge till death. Representative, Dakota Legislature, 1876. Married. 1870, April 22, Coralyn Augusta, daughter of Enoch and Catherine (Codington) Chamberlain, of Syracuse, N. Y. Had no children. Died of consumption, at his mother's home in Ann Arbor, Mich., 1878, Sept. 19, aged 40 years. 343,588,2968
