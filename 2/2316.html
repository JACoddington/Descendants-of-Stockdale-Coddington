  General Notes:

"He was born near Mt Horeb, in Warren Township, and lived on the home estate until he attained his majority. He is the son of Archibald and Mary Coddington, and grandson of Isaac Coddington, who was the first settler of the name in the township. Learning the trade of a blacksmith of his brother Isaiah, he worked with Isaiah in partnership for several years, and in 1834 purchased a farm in Bernard Township. This farm he sold in 1839, and bought another in Warren, where he resided until 1861, having in the mean time bought the mill property in Green Valley, where he then moved. His large flouring-mill was situated on Stoney Brook, near Plainfield, in a beautiful and romantic spot. A mill was built there in 1840 by John Binge, and was purchased by Archibald in 1854, who remodeled it in 1860, putting in a new waterwheel and new and improved machinery. The mill had three run of stones, and with its constant supply of water was a very desirable property, including in connection with it the blacksmith and repair shops, tenement house, and residences of the proprietors, father and son...John W. and Harrison were associated with their father in the milling interest in Green Valley" (Snell, 1881).

"In 1824 John Smith, and John, Benjamin, and Archibald Codington, united and built a small Methodist church at Mt. Horeb, and for many years it was kept up mainly by these families. The little congregation grew and prospered and now have a large and handsome edifice." ((McGill,1915: 106). 830,1907
