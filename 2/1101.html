  General Notes:

Graduate of the State University of Illinois, with a degree in M. Litt. Assistant teacher in Palmer's Academy, Kansas City, Kan, 1881-1882; principal of public school at Elmhurst, IL, 1882-1883; principal of public school at Barrington, IL, 1883-1884; principal of public school at Cummings, IL, 1884-1887; in business in Kansas City, MO, 1887-1890; principal of Sheldon School, Chicago, IL, 1890-1892; principal of Knickerbocker School, Chicago, IL, 1892-1901; principal of Goudy School, Chicago, IL, 1901-1905; attended Leipzig University and traveled in Europe, 1905-1906; principal Talcott School, Chicago, IL, 1906-1907; principal Graeme-Stewart School, Chicago, IL, 1907-1913; president of Chicago Alumni Association for the University of Illinois, 1906; Republican candidate for Superintendent of Schools of Cook Co., IL, 1910; member of the official board of Rogers Parm M. E. church, Chicago, 1914; 32nd degree Mason; member of Chicago Philosophic Round Table.
