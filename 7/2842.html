  General Notes:

For many years before 1844, camp meetings were held annually in August at Poynter’s camp ground in southern Montgomery County, Kentucky. Generally three services were held each day at 11 a.m., 3 p.m., and 7 in the evenings. Great crowds assembled, all of whom rode on horseback, carriages and buggies being relatively unknown in the community. These meetings were eagerly anticipated each year as seasons of great religious interest by the church members and as great fun and curiosity by the less thoughtful portion of the community. The congregation was notified to assemble by blowing a long tin trumpet, which was done by Col. J. C. Orear in the huge stand large enough to hold a dozen preachers.
