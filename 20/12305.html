  General Notes:

He and his family left NY in about 1756 and were in Frederick Co., Virginia. David was the only child of Ludwig to stay in the Frederick Co. area. He bought the "Head Spring" farm at Summit Point , Virginia, now W. Virginia in 1762 and sold it in 1772, when he bought "Glen Owen Farm". This homestead is still standing in Clarke County, VA, three miles east of Berryville, just west of the Shenandoah River. The family once operated a ferry service across the Shenandoah River and the bridge across the river near the homestead is officially named the Castleman Ferry Bridge.

The 1790 census of Frederick Co., Virginia showed that his household consisted of ten white persons and four negro slaves. Two sons, James and Benjamin emigrated to what is now LaRue Co., KY.

In 1811, he sold this farm, retaining the burial ground where his wife was buried in 1808. In 1811, he bought "Rose Hill" farm at Wickliffe, where he lived until his death. He is buried on the Glen Owen Farm, Berryville, Clarke CO., VA.
