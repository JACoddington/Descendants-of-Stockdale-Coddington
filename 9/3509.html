Patricia Lynne CODDINGTON 133,2383

  • Born: 14 Dec 1946, Richmond, Wayne Co., Indiana, USA
  • Marriage (1): Kenneth Michael RUETH on 27 Dec 1972 in Richmond, Wayne Co., Indiana, USA

  General Notes:

Pat learned to play the flute and piccolo and marched in the school band at Richmond High School. She graduated from Richmond High School in 1965. In the fall of 1965 she entered Butler University in Indianapolis, IN. She participated in the marching band and was a member of Tau Beta Sigma, a national band honorary for women. In 1969 she graduated from Butler University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Elementary Education. In the fall of 1969 she began teaching at Nicholson Elementary, an inner-city school in Richmond. She taught the first grade for six years and in 1973 she earned her Master of Arts degree in Education from Ball State University in Muncie, IN. She retired from teaching full time in June of 1975 in anticipation of the birth of their first child. She continued to substitute teach and did remedial tutoring.
She spent over 20 years working with the Vacation Bible School at her church. She taught the high school Sunday School class at church for over 12 years and functioned as the church youth director. She was also a 4-H leader and a scout leader.
Pat loved to sew and quilt. Most of her quilting was done by machine and she completed over 100 quilts in varying sizes and patterns. Most of them were gifts to friends and family.
She loved working with children.
Pat met her husband Ken on a blind date arranged by a mutual friend in February of 1972. Their first date was a near disaster, but they went out again a month later. They were engaged in August of 1972 and married Dec 27, 1972. They were married at St. Andrew Catholic Church in Richmond by F. Clifford Vogelsang and Pastor (Lutheran) Eugene Langholz. While it was a traditional ceremony, it was new at St. Andrew. It was a Wednesday night ceremony and it was one of the first 2 religion/2 minister services at St. Andrew. In many ways the clergy were more nervous than the couple. (Pat Rueth)
