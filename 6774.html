  General Notes:

aka Muggs, Maggie, Muggie.
Attended a boarding school run by one of the Patterson uncles. Her parents were uneasy that she should be away from home during the Civil War and brought her home again, where Dr. Jeremiah Ayres asked B. J. Douthitt for her hand on Apr 25, 1863.

By the 1870 census, Hiram and Benjamin Jameson had died. Jeremiah and Margaret Eliza were household 145_145 in Elliston. At household 151_151 appears the following black family: Douthet, Amos 45, farm laborer, Agnes, 40 keeping house, Sarah 16, Mariah, 13, Elizabeth, 11, Malinda, 9, Sharlot, 7, Sily, 5, Wilson, 4, Elza, 3. Intervening neighbors were Elizabeth Miller, Chares Bolton, Price Covington, Woodson Larro?, Dudley Grigas?, and Ephraim Oldham.

After Jeremiah Ayres died, she and her remaining children (Nannie, Fletcher, Lee Greta, Hazel Prue) at home moved to Greencastle, Indiana. 255,3500,3501
