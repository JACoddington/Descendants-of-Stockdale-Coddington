  General Notes:

Mary Inman married Smith Coddington secretly prior to her departure with her father to England on June 17, 1844. When Henry Inman learned of the marriage in early 1845, he sent her back to her husband.
A well-known publisher, journalist and firend of her father Henry, Nathaniel P. Willis, published an article stating that Mary had written a novel before she was 16. She then wrote Willis to say it was not true, and their correspondence continued for a few months. A rival publisher, James Watson Webb, acquired her letters, and accused Willis of seducing her, that her reputation was ruined, and that Willis was a cad. Meanwhile, Mary's parents learned of this correspondence, and tried several times to get Webb to return the letters. He did not, and it was a major scandal in New papers in the summer of 1851. 1026,1029
