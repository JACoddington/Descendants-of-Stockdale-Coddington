  General Notes:

Elder in Presbyterian Church at Union City, IN. Retail Shoe Dealer. republican. In 1861 enlisted in Co. C of 2nd Ohio Volunteer Infantry [Civil War]. Discharged at end of the year on account of ill health. (per Rev. Herbert G. Coddington).

Short History of the 2nd Ohio
When Confederate General Beaureguard fired on Fort Sumter, South Carolina in April 1861, President Lincoln put out a call for 75,000 troops from the States, to serve for 90 days.
In Ohio, there was a flurry of meetings, speeches and enlistments. Shortly there were 20 companies enlisted. They were split into the 1st and 2nd Regiments and on April 17,1861 they boarded trains for Washington City. Upon arrival at Washington City, they went into camp and the tedious repetition of training began.
Finally in July, the 2nd crossed into Virginia and participated in the 1st Battle of Bull Run. Here they served honorably, though they were not heavily engaged, and they left the night of the battle for home as their enlistments had expired.
Arriving back in Ohio nearly all of the 90 days men and officers reenlisted in the regiment for three years. Organized at Camp Dennison, they added new enlistees until they had nearly 900 men plus officers. In September 1861 under the command of a former Naval Lt-Commander, Brigadier General William "Bull" Nelson, the 2nd left Ohio, crossed the river at Cincinnati and moved south through Paris, Mount Sterling and into Olympian Springs, Kentucky. Here they camped and patrolled the roads looking for bands of recruits moving south to join the rebel cause. While in this area they were to first meet one James J. Andrews, a native Virginian loyal to the Union.
