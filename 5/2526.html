  General Notes:

He was also known as Orrear.6 He resided in 1726/27 at Stafford County, Virginia.7 He was a voter in 1741 at Prince William County, Virginia.8 He was shown on the tax list in 1759 at Fauquier County, Virginia, in the list created by Thomas Marshall.9

In 1761 John O'Rear and George Lathum were sued in trespass by Elizabeth Marshall, a neighbor of John O'Rear's (and probably related to the Elizabeth Marshall who married John O'Rear's grandson, Jesse) with the specification that they “---- with force and arms, to wit with clubs strike, whip &c one negro man named Joe belonging to the said plaintiff did assault beat would &c treat so that the aforesaid negro slave could not possibly attend or perform the lawful business of his said mistress whereby the same remains undone and she was deprived and lost the benefit and advantage of the labours of the said negro slave for a long time ----- they the said defendants then and those did to the said plaintiff against the peace of our soverign Lord the now King & to the damage of the said plaintiff twenty pounds.” The jury found for the plaintiff but apparently was unimpressed by the damage to the “King’s Peace” or Ms. Marshall’s loss of Joe’s services -- they awarded her one penny damages.10 On 26 July 1792 John O'Rear was sent a letter by a neighbor, an unknown person , who reported suspicious actions by an overseer slave belonging to John O'Rear.10

John O'Rear was blind before 1805.11 He was a planter before 1805 at Prince William County, Virginia.5 John O'Rear was enumerated with Josiah Anderson Orear on 1 June 1880 on the Census at Germantown, Kentucky.12 1073


John married Hester RENO, daughter of Francis RENNOE and Unknown, about 1742. (Hester RENO was born about 1720 in , Prince William Co., Virginia, USA 1073 and died after 18 Feb 1763 in , , Virginia, USA 1073.)
