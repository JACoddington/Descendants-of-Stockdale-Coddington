  General Notes:

Has the Family Bible.
"An "M. CODDINGTON" appears on page 1 of the 1855 Grant County, Wisconsin state census, Clifton Tp, per census index on Ancestry.com. This would be shortly after he m. Olive Manchester in that county, 1854.
A Matthew O. CODDINGTON voted in the first Colorado Territorial Election on August 19, 1861, residing in District 3 Precinct 6, Idaho District, voter #NN-226. Records published and on file at the Denver Public Library and online.
On the 1870 Idaho Springs, Clear Creek County, CO census, Matthew's occupation was given only as "livery stable", in which he was actually partners with Henry PLUMMER, enumerated on the line below Matthew. (FYI--this livery stable is still there (2004), has been preserved and now used for an attorney's offices.)
Apparently M. O. CODDINGTON ran for and was elected to the Colorado legislature in 1878, per the following excerpt from "Clear Creek & Boulder Valleys, Colorado History, 1880", p. 515: "...Mr. Graham is a Democrat in politics, and ran on that ticket for the Legislature in 1878, but was defeated by a small majority by M. O. Coddington, with the rest of his ticket." (Online at Ancestry.com, April 2004) Additional information is from the Colorado Miner newspaper, Georgetown, Clear Creek County, CO, September 14, 1878:
"THE REPUBLICANS
County Convention Proceedings."
"....For (state) representatives, a ballot between H. Montague and M.O. Coddington of Idaho, resulted in the nomination of Mr. Coddington." The same article noted that he was also appointed as a member of the County Executive Committee (representing the Idaho precinct). Digitized newspaper images online at the Colorado Historic Newspaper Collection, March 2005.
Shortly after Matthew's death date, on December 16, 1879 Matthew O. CODDINGTON and Henry PLUMMER received a land patent as follows, for which Matthew must have qualified before his death:
Mineral Patent-Lode, 5.17 acres "Solid Muldoon Lode", being in Section3 Township 4S Range 73W, 6th PM, in Clear Creek Colorado (per BLM Land Patent site, online at <http://www.glorecords.blm.gov/Patent Search>. In the photograph collection of the Denver Public Library there is a photo of the imposing home of "Mrs. Henry PLUMMER" ca 1890s, so Matthew's remaining partner must have done well at some time or another. (See "Idaho Springs Walking Tour," a publication of the Historical Society of Idaho Springs.)" 83,1712
