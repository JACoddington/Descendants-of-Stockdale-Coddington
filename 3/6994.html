  General Notes:

Supposedly wrote two letters to her Quaker congregation that were read during the meeting:
"Dear Friends,--Be faithful in ye service of God and min ye Lord's business, be diligent and bring ye power of ye Lord over all those that have gainsaid it; ande all you that be faithful go to visit them all that have been convinced from house to house, that if possible you may not have a hoofe in Egypt, and so every one go to seek ye lost sheep and bring him home on your back to ye Fold, and there will be more joy of that one sheep than of the ninety-nine in the Fold."

and

"And Friends all take heed of sleeping, sotishness, and dulness in Meetings for it is an illsavory thing to see one sit noddeing in a meeting, & so to loose ye sense of ye Lord & shamefac'dness; and it grieveth ye upwright and watchful, that wait upon ye Lord, to se such things and for ye Priest's people and others that come into your Meetings, to swe you that come together to worship God and wait upon him to have fellowship in His Spirit, for you to sit nodding is a shame and unseemly thing." (Clayton, 1882: 575). 73
