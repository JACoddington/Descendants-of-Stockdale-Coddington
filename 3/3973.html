  General Notes:

Born at 12 Bond St., christened in St. Bartholomew's P. E. Church, and died at 44 East 66th St.
Col. 2th Regiment NGNY Capt. US Vol. in Civil War. Commissioned 2nd Lieut. 51st. Regiment NY Vols, attached to Burnside's Corps. In all engagements near Richmond, brevet captain prior to Antietam (wounded, Capt. on staff of General Potter). Wounded Jackson, Mississippi, participated in siege of Knoxville, contracted typhoid fever. Resigned from Army due to broken health. Studied law but never practiced, devoted time to looking after his estates and those of his family. Col. several years after war.
Listed as "farmer" owner of a farm of 75 acres in Kingston, NY
Will of Jonathan C. Ayers (Ayres) of New York, NY, dated 6 Jan 1853, proved 11 May 1853 by the executor Jonathan I. Coddington (uncle), bequeathed to Clifford Coddington, youngest son of Jonathan I. Coddington, 160 acres of land in Ingham Co., Michigan, deed ed to me by Ann Coddington and described as southeast quarter of section 34 in Township One, North of Range Two, East, in the distribution of lands subject to sale at Detroit. 2483
