  General Notes:

Gilbert received bachelor of architecture and architectural engineering degrees from Ohio State University in 1931. He received a fellowship to Lake Forest Foundation for Architecture and Landscape Architecture, and earned a master of science degree in architecture from Columbia University with honors in 1932. From 1934-1942 he was the instructor of architecture at Ohio State University. From 1942-1946 he was a professor of engineering, also at Ohio State University. In 1946, he teamed up with a childhood friend, TW Brooks, and formed the architectural firm of Brooks and Coddington which lasted until 1969. This team won national acclaim for their designs. To name a few: St Stephens Episcopal Church in downtown Columbus, Ohio; the Berrington Wall and the Bell Sound Building in Worthington, Ohio. Gilbert (Gil) Coddington was responsible for the redesign/refurbishment of the Orange Johnson House in Worthington (Ohio) which currently houses the Worthington Historical Society. Virtually everything attached to him has either been listed on the National Register or has been written about extensively, used as examples of fine workmanship.
(Gayla Bumgarner, St. Paris, Ohio, Feb 2001)

He returned to Ohio State in 1961 as an architectural design professor and, when he retired in 1977, became a professor emeritus. He was affiliated with the Columbus, Ohio, chapter of the American Institute of Architects and was its president in 1949. He also belonged to Architects Society of Ohio, serving as its president in 1961; and the Ohio Historical Site Preservation Advisory Board, serving as its chairman from 1968 to 1972. He was named a distinguished alumnus of Ohio State's College of Engineering and Tau Beta Pi Fellows of the American Institute of Architects. He assisted in the publication of two books, engineering Drawings and Architect's Handbook-Bendix Home Appliances Inc. Architectural designs by his partnership were featured in 14 periodicals. (ex Detroit News, Dec 24, 1995)
