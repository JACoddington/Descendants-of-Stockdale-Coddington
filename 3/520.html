  General Notes:

Born at 56 White St., baptized at St. Bartholomew's P. E. Church, died at "St. Albans," buried in Lots 4309-10, Arbor Plot, Woodlawn Cemetery. First marriage in Fifth Ave. Pres. Church.
Jefferson and his older brother Edmund Randolph went to California in 1850. One of the ships was the Hannah Sprague.

Lived with first wife for four years before separating in 1858. Filed for divorce in New York and New Jersey, finally granted in Lake Co., Indiana in 1870. Coddington v. Coddington states, in part, "...said parties lived together as man and wife until July, 1858...that defendant then deserted the plaintiff and now not only refuses to return to him but said defendant refuses even to speak with him or have anything to do with him whatever...
and...
Defendant has for more than ten years been separated from her husband, that soon after the marriage of plaintiff and defendant, she found that she could not live with him in the relation of husband and wife, so utterly incompatible were they in their temperaments and tastes; to have done so would have been to the total destruction of the happiness of the defendant; she therefore abandoned the plaintiff and has ever since continued such abandonment."

Note: the Ellis Island database (cf. sources) records a Jefferson Coddington, no occupation, age 62, traveling first class on the The Kronprinzes Cecilie from Southampton, England to New York City, arriving 5 Oct. 1909. This person traveled again, age 63, in first class, this time with Emily Coddington, age 25, on the Mauretania from Liverpool, England to New York City, arriving 6 Sep 1912. The latter record gives his date of birth as 30 Jan. 1859, hers as 21 Oct 1882 (her true date of birth was 21 Oct. 1873) and their residence as 24 W. 58th St., New York. However, the real Jefferson Coddington died in 1876. 878,879,880,881
