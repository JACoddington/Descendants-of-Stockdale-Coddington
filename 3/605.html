  • Born: 30 Jun 1902, Nice, , Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur, France
  • Died: 10 May 1991, Bordentown, Burlington Co., New Jersey, USA at age 88

   Cause of his death was Acute cerebrovascular accident.

  General Notes:

Born in Villa Beaujolin, Route de Villefranche.
He was a renowned genealogist who was widely published. He was a member of the American Genealogy Society Hall of Fame.
He was educated at various schools in Switzerland and England till 1915; then at Carlton Academy, Summit, NJ (1915-1920); and at Harvard University (A. B., 1924; A.M., 1929). He traveled round the world, 1926-28 (2 full years); and spent the summer of 1930 in the USSR. He was an instructor in History and English at Harvard, 1930-33; and in History only, 1933-36. He was awarded the Woodbury Lowery Traveling Fellowship in Europe for the year 1936-37, and worked in London, Munich, Rome, Paris and Lisbon. He was a Professor of History at Olivet College, Olivet, Michigan, 1939-42, and was Chairman of the History Department there. He became an employee of the War Department in the summer of 1942. (per John I. Coddington).
In 1940 he was elected a Fellow of the American Society of Genealogists, in 1989 received the NEHGS Award of Merit, and in 1997 was elected posthumously to the Hall of Fame of the National Genealogical Society. 937,938,939,940
