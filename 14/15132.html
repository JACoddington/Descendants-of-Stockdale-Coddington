  General Notes:

"David Jameson was no doubt born in Virginia of wealthy parents. Apparently that wealth spilled over to David, who, himself, was a man of considerable wealth and station, having had some education, himself. He is discovered on a poll taken at the Court house of Prince William County on the first Monday in November Eighteen hundred and twenty for Electors to choose a President and Vice President of the United States. He was listed as fifty years old.[2]. He also served in the House of Burgess David's grandfather, James Jameson, first settled in St. Paul's Parish, Stafford co., VA., a county that forms the southern border of Prince William. He was married to Sarah Jameson who died in 1716 and had the son, William Jameson, as shown in the above genealogy.

Mr. Jameson left a will probated January 462, 1794 in Prince William co. He left his plantation to his sons, a slave each to each daughter, plus two additional plantations, furniture, stock, and slaves for his wife, Pattey Jameson, who he left as executrix. The will was witnessed by William Monroe , Nathaniel Ralls , Enoch Reno , George Edwards , and William Ashmore .
